# never

- **Never invent demand you cannot point at in the candidate dump.** If the argument needs
  a fact you don't have, the argument fails — do not supply the fact.
- **Never sort or gate candidates by engagement.** Upvotes are a generic-problem detector;
  the 12-upvote post about a specific broken workflow is the one worth building for.
  Engagement is weak secondary evidence, never the gate.
- **Never merge `mine.md` and `others.md`.** One league would compare certainty-of-problem
  against certainty-of-market, and an n=1 friction entry would lose to a 40-upvote post
  every night.
- **Never let `idea-engine` read `profile.md` or `goals.md`.** See the personalization line
  in the kernel.
- **Never gate a friction entry on corroboration.** `n=1` is information (no market), not a
  failure (the problem is still real). Annotate; do not block.
- **Never score.** No 1–10, no weighted axes, no composite. Rank by argument.
- **Never put PII in `hq-os`.** It is public. *(Belt-and-braces — `guard.yml` is the
  actual guarantee.)*
- **Never leave AI attribution in a commit or a file.** *(Belt-and-braces — `guard.yml`
  again.)*
- **Never append to a full list.** Ten slots. Entry is by displacement or not at all.
- **Never read the inbox.** Gmail is send-only. `applied` owns job mail; the briefing never
  touches it.
